About

A Friday kitchen with deep Maastricht roots

Born at Landbouwbelang, Foodbank Maastricht has grown through years of hands-on care, donated produce, and Friday evenings around one long table.

Origin story

Foodbank Maastricht was founded over 12 years ago at Landbouwbelang, a cultural community space in Maastricht. What began as a practical response to food waste became a steady weekly ritual of collecting, cooking, sharing, and learning together.

How it works

Foodbank Maastricht 101

From market leftovers to Friday dinner. The recipe changes every week because the ingredients do too. The rhythm stays simple: collect, cook, eat.

3:00 PM

Collect surplus produce

Everything comes from market stands: surplus produce that's perfectly good to eat, saved before it gets thrown away.

4:00 PM

Cook together

Return to Landbouwbelang, see what the market gave us, decide the menu on the spot, and start cooking together.

8:00 PM

Come to the table

Dinner is communal, donation-based, and vegan. You do not need to know anyone in advance, just show up.

Hosted by Landbouwbelang

Landbouwbelang provides the space where surplus produce becomes dinner. Its community spirit makes room for a kitchen that belongs to everyone and keeps the Friday tradition visible in the city.
